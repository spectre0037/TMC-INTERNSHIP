--- ANSWERS ---
1. Enterprise software is programs built to run an entire organization's operations rather than one person's task — HR, finance, inventory, sales, all connected. An ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) system is the specific type that ties those departments into one shared database, so one transaction updates everywhere automatically.
2. SAP is a German company and the world's biggest maker of ERP software. A "system integrator / SAP partner" is a company that installs, configures, connects, and supports SAP for a client rather than building the software itself.
3. TMC (TallyMarks Consulting) was founded in 2010 and is headquartered in Lahore. It's known as Pakistan's largest SAP partner for six consecutive years, serving 320+ clients across Pakistan, UAE, KSA and the USA. Recent achievement: named a Global Winner at the 2026 SAP Partner Awards.
4. AiNow is TMC's own description of itself as "not a product, not a platform — a movement": a commitment to making every organization AI-enabled and AI-first. In practice it also functions as their AI-powered analytics offering, turning business data into real-time insights for faster, smarter decisions.
5. Digitizing a process means putting a manual task (paper, spreadsheets) into software — faster, less error-prone, but still just recording what happened. Making it intelligent means the software also interprets and acts: flags anomalies, predicts outcomes, recommends decisions — anticipating rather than just reporting.
6. Example: Accounts Payable. A normal ERP just records invoices and payments. AI on top can read incoming invoices, flag duplicates or fraud, match them to purchase orders, and auto-approve routine ones — turning a manual finance task into something largely self-running.
